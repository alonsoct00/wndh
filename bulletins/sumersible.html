SUBMERSIBLE first reading in the light

December 2, 2021

Ana Garcia and Lane Dombois' second musical Submersible received its first reading in May, marking the shows first exposure to outside ears in years.

The show—which's been brewing since 2014 passively, 2017 actively—finds a young woman, Andee, growing among and apart from her siblings as they confront adulthood, so called. How support can be a crutch. One's gravitational center can be one's hell. Etc.

So she takes to the undersea, Andee, and is met with the tumult of a humpback whale migration aggravated by a changing ocean. Leagues of desolation are a lot for a humpback whale to accept, and completely unnavigable by a young woman. Seemingly. And approach and acceptance are different.

We read the show online with the help of beautiful collaborators and friends (some of whom are working on other WNDH projects), playing MIDI versions of Ana's otherly-operatic nautical-connubial score. Recognizing oneself more than ever in the ever-moving surface of the sea. Unfamiliar, but internal.

Authoring continues, as do questions about how to represent humpback whales on a stage–and, most importantly, why is anyone worried about such a trivially simple problem?
